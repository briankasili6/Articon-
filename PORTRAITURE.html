PHOTOGRAPHY

A clear, sharp reference photo is essential to produce a portrait that captures the details and characters of the one involved. This is not always easy, or sometimes even possible. Below are some of the aspects to consider when taking or selecting a photo that will be used as a reference for your portrait:
  • Take the highest resolution photo possible uing a digital camera or new model smart phone. Else otherwise a photographer will be provided for you.
  • Shoot outside in natural daylight, or if indoors, next to a window or under a skylight for accurate colour. Avoid direct sunlight as extreme glare tends to wash out details. Likewise, overly shaded locations with no light will cast too heavy a shadow.
  • The photo should be taken the same level/eye level and the area to be prtraited should fit the frame.
  • When zooming in, we can see good detail in the face and reflection in the eyes. If you can see the individual hairs, it is likely to be considered a suitable quality.
  • Portraits based on a composite of multiple different photos, i.e, eyes from one photo and mouth from other photo is not recommended
  • Bear in Mind, a portrait should not be an exact copy of a photo but just a handmade work.
